Efficient simulation of time-periodic flows: A time-periodic space-time method with application to moving hydrofoils and idea's for Projection-based Reduced Order Modelling

JacobLotz May 4th, 2023, 16h15, Lecture Hall K

Forced time-periodic flows are ubiquitous in a large number of industrial applications. Prototypical examples include tidal energy converters, wave energy converters and propellers. The computation of time-periodic flows requires an excessively long simulation before it reaches a periodic state, giving high computational costs. We need an efficient method to solve these flows to allow for fast design optimization and real-time simulations. First, we introduce an approach to shorten the size of the time domain such that we only have to compute the flow in one period of the forced motion. The method allows for efficient computation of viscous flows past periodically moving objects. Second, we show ideas and progress for a Projection-based Reduced Order Model (PROM) using the model we implemented in the first part. After an expensive start-up phase, we can evaluate these PROMs at low costs.

The M&TT Colloquia is a colloquium series that is organized within the department of Maritime and Transport Technology at Delft University of Technology. The organization is done by PhD students from this department.
